Opportunity OS — Organization Detail Screen (Hero System)
Purpose and Goals

The Organization Detail screen acts as the hero interface for each account.
Its primary goals are to:

Provide a consolidated view of an organization’s health, risk, and progress across all open opportunities.
Surface data‑driven next best actions that drive execution and revenue.
Highlight coverage gaps and relationship mapping to inform targeted outreach.
Display a chronological activity timeline and pending tasks, bridging CRM storage with actionable intelligence.

This screen replaces the static “organization info” page with a dynamic control center that guides users through what to do next and why.

Layout Overview

The page follows a mobile‑first, card‑based layout consistent with the current dark theme (as seen in the existing dashboard and list views). Each section uses a card with soft corners and ample padding.
Here is the recommended structure, top to bottom:

Header
Organization name and icon.
Account state label (e.g. Cold, Warming, Active, At Risk, Expanding) as a colored badge.
Quick access icons for editing organization info or navigating to the parent account.
Intelligence Pulse Card
A 2×2 grid summarising:
Health (overall engagement score).
Risk (probability of churn or lost deal).
Gaps (number of missing roles/contacts).
Focus (active vertical or strategic tier).
Each metric uses icons and progress bars for quick scanning.
Tapping a metric opens a detailed modal explaining how it is calculated.
Relationship Map & Coverage Gaps
Split into two columns:
Key Stakeholders: list of contacts grouped by role type (Decision Maker, Influencer, Champion). Each row shows name, title, influence level, last engagement date and relationship strength (visualised with a bar or dot indicator). Tapping a contact opens the contact detail page.
Coverage Gaps: automatically detected missing roles (e.g. “No champion identified”) and weaknesses (“Only one influencer; need at least two”). Each gap suggests an action such as “Identify champion” or “Schedule coffee with influencer”.
A CTA (“Add Contact”) allows quick creation of new contacts from this section.
Pipeline Summary
A horizontal scrollable list of all open opportunities tied to this organization. Each card contains:
Opportunity name.
Current stage and probability.
Deal value.
Days in stage (with red/yellow/green indicators).
Tapping a card opens the opportunity detail screen where the user can move the stage or adjust probability.
Primary & Supporting Actions
The system’s recommendation engine chooses a single primary action based on inactivity, stage stagnation and gaps (e.g. “Call John to discuss referral agreement – opportunity stalled for 6 days”).
Underneath, two or three supporting actions are offered as alternatives (e.g. “Schedule meeting with CFO”, “Send thank‑you email”).
Each action includes an impact statement answering “why now” and “why this account” to help the user prioritise.
Activity Timeline & Task List
A timeline feed showing recent activities (calls, emails, meetings) and automated actions. Each entry lists the activity type, timestamp, outcome and the user who performed it.
A tabs control toggles between Activity and Tasks.
Tasks lists both manual and automated tasks associated with this organization, ordered by due date. Each task shows title, priority, due date and status (open, in progress, completed). Tasks generated by follow‑up automation indicate their source (e.g. “Generated due to inactivity”).
Users can mark tasks complete or reschedule directly from this list.
Additional Info (Optional collapsing section)
Details about the organization’s vertical, strategic tier, expansion maturity and account structure type.
Links to parent and child organizations, showing where this account sits in the hierarchy.
Notes, attachments and documents (e.g. business cards, proposals) related to the account.
Data and Logic Requirements
Account State Label

The account state (Cold, Warming, Active, At Risk, Expanding) is determined by a combination of:

Metric	Description
Engagement Recency	Time since last meaningful activity with any contact.
Opportunity Velocity	Days in current pipeline stage vs. expected days.
Relationship Coverage	Presence of required roles (decision maker, influencer, champion).
Account Score	Calculated composite score reflecting health, risk and gaps.

States should be colour‑coded for immediate recognition (e.g. blue for cold, amber for warming, green for active, red for at risk, purple for expanding).

Intelligence Pulse Metrics

Each metric is calculated as follows:

Metric	Inputs	Calculation
Health	Contact engagement recency, volume of activities, opportunity progression	Normalised score from 0–100.
Risk	Inactivity duration, opportunity age, churn signals, negative activities	Percentage chance of deal or relationship loss.
Gaps	Number of mandatory roles unfilled, total contacts vs. desired coverage	Count or percentage of unfilled roles.
Focus	Active vertical overlay (e.g. Healthcare, GovCon), strategic tier, expansion maturity	Textual summary; pick the highest‑priority vertical overlay attached to this organization.
Primary Action Engine

The recommendation algorithm should consider:

Opportunity stagnation: days in stage exceeding threshold.
Inactivity: time since last engagement with any key stakeholder.
Coverage gaps: missing roles or poor relationship strength.
Pipeline priorities: opportunities with highest value or probability.
Account state: at‑risk accounts receive urgent actions; active accounts receive growth actions.

The primary action should always be paired with a why statement explaining the context.

Coverage Gap Detection

Define required roles per account type (e.g. Decision Maker, Champion, Influencer).
Use the relationship schema (role type, influence level, relationship strength, engagement recency) to determine which roles exist and which are missing or weak.
Flag any missing or weak roles and provide suggestions (e.g. “Identify champion – current champion last engaged 90 days ago”).

Pipeline Summary

Pull data from the Opportunity schema:

Field	Display	Notes
Stage	Show stage name; colour‑code by probability bracket	
Value	Currency and value of the deal	
Probability	Percentage; base on current stage default but allow overrides	
Days in Stage	Calculated; highlight when exceeding threshold	
Activity & Task System

Leverage the existing Activity and Task schemas. Show activity type (call, email, meeting), timestamp and outcome. For tasks, display priority, due date, status and source trigger.
Allow inline actions to mark tasks complete or create a new follow‑up task.

Implementation Guidance
Frontend
Use React with TypeScript to implement the screen as a functional component (e.g. OrganizationDetail.tsx).
Build modular subcomponents (e.g. IntelligenceCard, RelationshipList, PipelineCard, ActionSection, ActivityTimeline) for clarity and reuse.
Employ tailwindcss for layout and spacing. Use shadcn/ui elements like Card, Badge, Tabs, Button, Table and Avatar to ensure consistency with existing screens.
Design responsive behaviour: on larger displays (tablet/desktop), use a two‑column layout where the timeline and tasks occupy the right column; on mobile, stack vertically.
Keep backgrounds dark and text high‑contrast to match current aesthetic.
Backend / Data Fetching
Fetch organization data from the workspace database via API endpoints or context providers.
Implement a service that aggregates metrics for the intelligence pulse and calculates account state.
Expose endpoints to retrieve relationships, pipeline opportunities, activities and tasks filtered by organization ID.
Provide mutation endpoints to create/edit contacts, tasks and opportunities from this screen.
Logic Layer
Develop the recommendation engine as a separate module that takes organization metrics and returns a list of recommended actions with impact statements.
Use parameterised thresholds (e.g. expected days in each stage, inactivity duration) to make recommendations flexible and easy to adjust.
Ensure that actions are suggestions only and do not auto‑execute (aligns with intelligence governance rules).
Future Enhancements
Integrate scoring workflows (account, contact, opportunity scores) once the scoring system matures.
Add charts to visualise trends over time (e.g. engagement frequency or pipeline velocity) using recharts.
Hook into vertical overlays (GovCon, Healthcare) to surface domain‑specific metrics and recommendations.
Conclusion

This specification transforms the static organization page into an actionable control center.
By aligning the user experience with your execution‑driven vision, it guides users towards the next best action, helps them understand why it matters, and builds a stronger data moat through structured interactions.
